coraldefense

Coraldefense is Emily Mo, an experimental pop musician based in the Boston area.

In her 2022 album Housefly Sting, Coraldefense blends glitchy electronics with silky, dreamy synth textures, pulling influences from artists like Broadcast, Sweet Trip, and Sheena Ringo. Housefly Sting lyrically explores the chaotic ripple effect of human interactions and the ironic emptiness of the infinitely resourceful internet.

"Hypnogogic shapes emerge in the hypnotic leads and shadowy chord progressions giving housefly sting a sense of timelessness and wonder. There are so many interesting ideas here and the narrative sequence heightens their impact." - Foxy Digitalis

bio

Coraldefense is Emily Mo, a Boston-based experimental pop musician who sonically illustrates the visions in her mind’s eye with distorted synth textures and tight electronic rhythms. Emily tought herself to play the piano as a child, but quickly found that she enjoyed creating her own songs more than learning to play others’ songs, and has been making music ever since. Originally a bedroom songwriter with a passion for oddball DIY sound design, she eventually shifted heavily towards electronic production in order to sculpt more vivid, wondrous sonic textures.
